My work sits at the intersection of quantum field theory, string theory, and pure mathematics. Below are some highlights.

---

## Positivity and Complete Monotonicity in QFT

Scattering amplitudes and Feynman integrals satisfy infinitely many positivity constraints rooted in the mathematical notion of **complete monotonicity**. With Johannes Henn, I showed how these properties connect to **positive geometries** and **dual volumes**, opening new avenues for analytically constraining and numerically approximating Feynman integrals.

📄 [JHEP04(2025)150](https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP04(2025)150) · [arXiv:2512.18499](https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.18499) · [arXiv:2509.02239](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.02239)

## Cosmological Amplitudes via Ramanujan's Master Theorem

Computing tree-level cosmological correlators (in-in correlators and wavefunction coefficients) in FRW spacetimes is notoriously difficult. With Qinglin Yang, I developed a systematic method based on **massive family trees** and a generalization of **Ramanujan's master theorem**, yielding infinite series solutions and Feynman-like diagrammatic rules.

📄 [JHEP04(2026)036](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.13126)

---

## Celestial Holography Meets the S-matrix Bootstrap

Using variables inspired by **celestial amplitudes**, Sudip Ghosh, Aninda Sinha and I discovered a new positivity property tied to spin-0 partial wave dominance. This framework allowed us to exactly quantify **low spin dominance** and derive non-projective bounds on Wilson coefficients in scalar effective field theories.

📄 [JHEP08(2022)216](https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP08(2022)216)

---

## S-matrix Bootstrap and Geometric Function Theory

With Aninda Sinha, I established a surprising connection between the S-matrix bootstrap and **geometric function theory** (the Bieberbach conjecture and related mathematics). This led to **two-sided bounds** on Wilson coefficients for pions, photons, and gravitons — explaining why these coefficients are generically O(1) and systematically characterizing low spin dominance.

📄 [JHEP12(2021)203](https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP12(2021)203) · [SciPost Phys. 13 (2022) 051](https://doi.org/10.21468/SciPostPhys.13.3.051)

## Positive Geometries Beyond Planar N=4 SYM

The amplituhedron program — connecting scattering amplitudes to the geometry of positive spaces — was originally developed for planar N=4 super Yang-Mills and bi-adjoint φ³ theory. I extended this to **φ⁴ interactions** (Stokes polytopes) and general **φᵖ theories**, vastly broadening the class of theories with known positive geometries.

📄 [JHEP10(2019)271](https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP10(2019)271) · [JHEP08(2019)067](https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP08(2019)067)

---

## Generalized Permutahedra and Stringy Amplitudes

With Song He, Zhenjie Li, and Chi Zhang, I introduced **generalized permutahedra** as a new family of binary geometries. These give rise to large classes of "stringy" amplitudes sharing key properties with string theory: exponential UV softness and factorization at finite α'.

📄 [JHEP10(2020)054](https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP10(2020)054)

---

## Holographic Conformal Blocks via Wilson Networks

With Atanu Bhatta and Nemani Suryanarayana, I developed a prescription for computing **conformal partial waves** holographically using gravitational Wilson networks in the first-order (Chern-Simons-like) formulation of AdS₃ gravity — complementing the geodesic Witten diagram method in metric gravity.

📄 [JHEP06(2016)119](https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP06(2016)119) · [JHEP12(2018)125](https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP12(2018)125)

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the **Max Planck Institute for Physics** in Munich, working with [Johannes Henn](https://www.mpp.mpg.de/en/research/theoretical-physics/).

My research explores hidden mathematical structures in quantum field theory. I work on **scattering amplitudes**, **positive geometries**, and the **S-matrix bootstrap** — uncovering connections between physics and areas of mathematics including geometric function theory, combinatorics, and the theory of positive definite functions.

Recent directions include:
- Positivity properties (complete monotonicity) of scattering amplitudes and Feynman integrals
- Cosmological correlators and their computation via novel analytic methods
- Two-sided bounds on effective field theories using geometric function theory
- Positive geometries for scalar interactions beyond planar N=4 SYM

Previously, I held a postdoctoral position at the **Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru** (2021–2023) with [Aninda Sinha](https://chep.iisc.ac.in/Personnel/asinha.html), and completed my PhD at the **Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai** (2014–2019) under [Nemani V. Suryanarayana](https://www.imsc.res.in/~nemani/). I have also held visiting positions at the Institute for Advanced Study (Princeton) and the Institute of Theoretical Physics (Beijing).
